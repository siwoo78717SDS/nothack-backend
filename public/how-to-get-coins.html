What are coins?

Coins are the in-game currency for Zeropoint. You use them to buy feature unlocks in the Feature Shop, like Direct Messages, Group Chat, and Image Upload.

You can’t buy coins with real money. You earn them by being active and using the site in smart ways.

Daily

1. Daily logins & activity

Just using the site regularly is one of the easiest ways to gain coins. Check in often, explore pages, chat, and complete tasks.

  • Come back every day and stay active.
  • Try to complete at least one meaningful action (chat, level up, etc.).
  • Keep an eye on new features that may reward activity.

Tip: Combine daily logins with other methods below to grow your coins steadily.

Daily

2. Play games / complete tasks (if enabled)

Some deployments of Zeropoint include mini games or missions that reward coins when you finish them.

  • Check the Tasks or Missions sections (if available).
  • Look for any labels like “+Coins” or “Reward”.
  • Finish simple quests every day to stack small rewards.

Not every server has the same game setup. Your admin can toggle certain rewards on or off.

One-time

3. Achievements & milestones

When you hit important milestones, you might get coin rewards from the admin:

  • Reaching a new account level.
  • Helping test new features.
  • Special events run by admins or teachers.

If you’re not sure which achievements exist, ask your teacher or admin. Some of them might give bonus coins or other unlocks.

Special

4. Admin / teacher rewards

On some servers, admins might reward users with coins for things like:

  • Helping others in chat or group discussions.
  • Reporting serious bugs or security problems responsibly.
  • Making useful suggestions that improve the system.

5. What doesn't give coins

  • Spamming messages
  • Trying to cheat.

Cheating or abusing the system can lead to losing coins or even being banned from using coins completely.

Next steps

  • Check your current coins on the Account page.
  • Visit the Feature Shop to see what you can unlock.
Coin rules can slightly change depending on how your server is configured. If you're unsure, ask your teacher or admin.
